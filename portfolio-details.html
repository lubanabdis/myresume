Abstract

After the COVID-19 pandemic, another infectious disease threat emerged, namely monkeypox with its other name, the monkeypox virus. Monkeypox is a disease transmitted through an animal virus that belongs to the genus Orthopoxvirus and has symptoms similar to those of smallpox and chickenpox. One way to diagnose monkeypox is by classifying similar skin lesion images to determine whether the patient is indeed suffering from monkeypox or not. The common method used for image classification is Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). CNN is a development of Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) that is designed to process two-dimensional data such as images and audio. The CNN architecture used in this study is VGG-19 with 16-convolution layers + relu, 5-pooling layers, and fully connected layers. The dataset used consisted of 1,000 images of skin lesions similar to the monkeypox virus to measure the level of accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity percentage. The testing results using unseen data obtained an accuracy rate of 76%, a sensitivity rate of 76%, and a specificity rate of 92%.
